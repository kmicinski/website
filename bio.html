--- title: All about Kris! ---

I first ventured into programming fairly young in life. This was not, of course, uncommon for young programmers in the 90s, and indeed a whole swarm of today's programmers started with the same Linux distribution and book on C programming as I. After that, I've been having as much as fun as possible solving interesting problems, writing in crazy languages, talking about math for long stretches of time, and playing a lot of the drums (not to mention, spending too much on cymbals).

After working on writing HTML pages for a while, I wondered what it might be like to write in Perl and control what went on "behind the scenes." I understand now that I learned programming backward: as I started with HTML, Perl, Basic (but just for a few days!), C, C++, Java, etc... I finally moved on to the newer collection of functional languages like Haskell, ML, OCaml, F#, and the related others. I have written in many languages and in many situations: from web based database driven applications, down to compilers for simplistic multicycle microcontrollers (the 68HC12, in that case). Eventually, I aspire to research and develop programming languages, especially those with richer type systems and more expressive (safer?) semantics. My main academic interests (besides languages) include computer architecture and systems.

I spent my younger years in a small town in north Texas, but moved to East Lansing, Michigan to attend college. I recently finished a degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from MSU by patching together a number of classes in a variety of subjects. While at MSU I was employed by the NeEWS laboratory, working on a variety of applications relating to sensor networks and mobile routing problems. The day to day activities consisted of programming in low level C code that runs on mobile sensor nodes, along with higher level code mainly written in Java. For the last year there I worked on a prototype of a publish subscribe wireless distribution system implemented on the Android platform (and I continue to do Android development from time to time). My coursework at MSU consisted mainly of computer science, a bit of electrical engineering, and a little bit of math.

After my second year at MSU I worked as an intern at Microsoft, where I worked on prototyping a domain specific language for user mode interrupt handling with the Windows Driver Foundation team. I still think this is a nice example of where PL ideas can be used to solve real problems, and I'd like to keep this spirit in my future research.

I now reside in Hollywood, and work in programming languags research with the PLUM people. (Jeff Foster and Mike Hicks are excellent researchers and teachers, and I would reccomend any prospective PL students to apply to UMD!) I spend most of my time listening to The Weepies, The Posies, The Mars Volta, Counting Crows, Marillion, fighting with TeX, Coq, and emacs, teaching undergrads about PL, tracking down bugs, reading papers (oh geeze, need to do more of that one), and thinking about how to make my desk more fasshionable.
